What is WEB3?

Web3 was the next evolution in the Internet, built using decentralized technologies such as blockchain. Whereas Web 2.0 was dominated by a relatively small number of centralized platforms, Web3 gives users ownership of their data, digital identity, and assets. Web3 employs peer-to-peer networks, smart contracts, and tokenization—in the form of cryptocurrency and NFTs—to enable direct, trustless interactions with no need for intermediaries. Web3 empowers more users through higher enrichment of privacy, financial inclusion, and new business models, such as the next-generation of DAOs with co-ownership at their core. This potentially paves the way for a more open, transparent, user-centric internet.

Blockchain Technology

Blockchain is one kind of decentralized, distributed digital ledger technology. Transactions are recorded across a network of many computers in a secure, transparent, and immutable manner. Unlike in traditional databases where a single entity is in control of the data, blockchain lives on a peer-to-peer network, and therefore, it is trustless in nature, and there is no requirement for an intermediary in verifying the transactions.
Every block in the chain contains a batch of transactions; once added to the chain of networks, each block is cryptographically sealed and thereby linked to the previous one such that the history record cannot be altered. Whereas the basic technology of blockchain was harnessed for cryptographic digital currencies upon the birth of Bitcoin, it has very extensive applications across many areas in which the need for trust, transparency, and security arises, like finance, supply chain management, and health.
